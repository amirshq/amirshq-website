Portfolio · Story 01

Milky Way above Filband.

Filband is a small village perched nearly 3,000 metres above sea level in northern Iran — a place where you look down at the clouds and up at the stars.

Theme · Altitude & wonder
Technique · Wide‑angle nightscape, natural rock framing
Feeling · Disbelief turned into gratitude

Standing among the weathered rocks of Filband at night, the Milky Way rose so vividly I could hardly believe my eyes. During the day this village offers a magnificent view of clouds rolling far below your feet; at night the sky takes over, and the galactic core appears to pour out of the rock formations themselves.

Portfolio · Story 07

Crepuscular rays over Yucatán.

On the road from Chichen Itza to Cancún, the setting sun split into radiant beams that stretched across the entire sky — crepuscular rays on one side, their fainter anticrepuscular counterparts on the other.

Theme · Atmospheric optics & road trips
Technique · Wide panorama at golden hour
Feeling · Pulling over because the sky demands it

Crepuscular rays appear when the sun sits low on the horizon and clouds or terrain cast long shadows through the atmosphere, creating ethereal beams of light. Anticrepuscular rays form on the opposite side of the sky — often fainter, sometimes a seamless extension of their counterparts, sometimes individual beams originating from natural structures like mountains. During my journey through Yucatán I had been chasing this phenomenon, and on this evening it finally unfolded in full: a breathtaking display of radiance that painted the highway sky in purple and pink.

Portfolio · Story 20

Lunar eclipse over the Biosphere.

On the frigid night of March 13–14, 2025, the Moon turned deep red as it drifted above the geodesic dome of the Montréal Biosphere — geometry meeting geometry in the winter sky.

Theme · Architecture & celestial alignment
Technique · Timed composite sequence
Feeling · Patience rewarded in freezing air

The total lunar eclipse of March 2025 was visible across eastern Canada on one of Montréal's coldest late-winter nights. I chose the Biosphere as the foreground — its lattice dome echoes the arc of the Moon's path, and the blood-red colour of totality contrasted beautifully against the steel framework. Each Moon in the sequence is a separate exposure, composited later to trace the full journey from partial shadow to deep totality and back.
